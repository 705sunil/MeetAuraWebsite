Child Safety Standards

Last updated: June 2026

Zero Tolerance Policy: MeetAura has a strict zero-tolerance policy toward child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE). Any content, behavior, or activity involving the sexualization, abuse, or exploitation of minors is strictly prohibited and will result in immediate account termination and reporting to the relevant authorities.

1. Our Commitment

MeetAura is committed to providing a safe platform for all users. We do not allow, tolerate, or facilitate any form of child sexual abuse material (CSAM) or child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE) on our app, in any form, for any reason.

MeetAura is intended for use only by individuals aged 18 and older. We do not knowingly permit minors to create or maintain accounts on our platform.

2. Prohibited Content & Conduct

The following are strictly prohibited on MeetAura and will result in immediate, permanent account suspension:

  • Any image, video, text, or content depicting or sexualizing a minor
  • Grooming, soliciting, or attempting to exploit a minor in any way
  • Sharing, requesting, or distributing CSAM in any format
  • Using the app to arrange contact with a minor for sexual purposes
  • Any attempt to extort, blackmail, or coerce a minor
  • Creating profiles that misrepresent age to target or deceive minors

3. Age Requirement

MeetAura requires all users to be 18 years of age or older to create an account. Profile location, identity-revealing matching, and in-person meeting features make this app unsuitable for minors. If we become aware that an account belongs to a user under 18, we will immediately terminate the account and delete all associated data.

4. Detection & Enforcement

We take the following measures to protect users from CSAE:

  • User reporting and blocking tools available on every profile
  • Manual review of reported content and accounts
  • Immediate, permanent suspension of any account found violating this policy
  • Full cooperation with law enforcement and reporting to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) or equivalent authorities where required by law

5. Reporting CSAE Content or Behavior

If you encounter any content, behavior, or account on MeetAura that you believe violates this policy or involves the exploitation of a minor, please report it immediately. We review all reports related to child safety as our highest priority.

🚨 Report a Concern

Reports are reviewed urgently and may be escalated directly to law enforcement.

Report to meetaura2026@gmail.com

6. In-App Reporting

Within the MeetAura app, users can:

  • Block any user directly from their profile page
  • Submit feedback or reports through the in-app Feedback feature
  • Contact support directly via the Settings screen

7. External Resources

If you or someone you know needs help related to child exploitation, please also contact:

  • National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC): report.cybertip.org
  • Internet Watch Foundation (IWF): www.iwf.org.uk
This policy is reviewed regularly and applies to all users, content, and activity on the MeetAura platform, in accordance with Google Play's Child Safety Standards requirements.
